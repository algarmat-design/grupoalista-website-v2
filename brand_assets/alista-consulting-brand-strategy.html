Visual Identity Direction

Logo Style Recommendation

Combination Mark — Abstract Symbol + Wordmark

A combination mark is the strategic choice for a consulting firm targeting owner-operators. The abstract symbol builds recognition and works at favicon/embossed scale. The wordmark ensures instant legibility for cold introductions. Together they establish both authority and approachability — essential for a brand where trust is the primary conversion mechanism.

Against monogram only: A two-letter mark (AC) works only after brand recognition is established. At this stage, name clarity beats symbol elegance.
Against wordmark only: Without a mark, the brand loses scalability at small sizes (business cards, social avatars, stamps). A mark anchors the brand at every touchpoint.

Visual Anti-Rules

What ALISTA CONSULTING Never Does Visually

01
Never use gradient backgrounds that "look like AI." Purple-to-indigo, rainbow mesh gradients, and neon-glow aesthetics signal genericity. Our gradients are subtle, directional, and dark.
02
Never fill the page. Negative space is not empty — it is the visual equivalent of confidence. A brand that needs to fill every pixel looks insecure. Premium brands breathe.
03
Never use stock photo clichés. No handshakes, boardroom tables, or laptop-on-coffee-shop images. If photography is used, it shows real operational environments: factories, warehouses, offices with people actually working.
04
Never use Prestige Gold as a background color. Gold is an accent — it appears in section numbers, metric highlights, and select emphasis. Applied as a background, it reads as cheap. Applied sparingly, it reads as earned.
05
Never animate decoratively. Every animation must serve comprehension or guide attention. Spinning logos, bouncing icons, and parallax-for-parallax's-sake undermine the gravitas of a firm that charges for operational discipline.

Pillar Three

5 Original Logo Concepts

Each concept is rendered as a working visual. All five are intentionally distinct — different in structure, symbolism, and typographic direction — to serve as true strategic alternatives, not iterations of the same idea.

Vertex

Combination Mark · Recommended
Visual Structure
Two converging lines form a sharp peak — the geometry of the letter A reduced to its essential form. A thin cyan crossbar at mid-height. A gold dot marks the apex. Below: wordmark in Syne 800 + DM Sans body.
Symbolic Meaning
The converging lines represent two forces meeting at a single point of maximum efficiency — client operations and AI-powered strategy. The peak is the outcome: measurable savings. The gold apex is the moment of realization.
Typographic Direction
Syne 800 for "ALISTA" — architectural weight that commands attention. Wide-tracked DM Sans 400 for "CONSULTING" — the qualification that grounds the authority.
Audience Resonance
Family business owners recognize the upward trajectory immediately. It's not abstract — it reads as progress. The clean geometry signals precision without feeling cold or corporate. The gold apex signals the reward.
Competitive Differentiation
Regional IT consultancies use circuit patterns, globes, and gears. This mark is architectural and minimal — it reads as a design-forward firm, not a technology vendor. Visually closer to McKinsey than to a local IT shop.

Signal

Abstract Mark
Visual Structure
Three ascending rectangular bars in green, sapphire, and cyan — heights escalating left to right. A minimal upward arrow above the tallest bar. Proportions echo signal-strength indicators but at a refined scale.
Symbolic Meaning
Green = current savings unlocked. Blue = strategy deployed. Cyan = full digital transformation achieved. The progression is the promise: every bar represents a phase of improvement. The arrow signals it never stops.
Typographic Direction
Syne 700 (lighter than Concept 1) for "ALISTA" — still authoritative but with more white space. Ultra-light DM Sans for "CONSULTING" — the restraint in weight contrasts with the bold bars above.
Audience Resonance
Immediately readable as "progress." No abstraction required. A business owner sees three bars going up and a number — it communicates measurable improvement without a single word.
Competitive Differentiation
Color-coded metrics embedded in a logo. Unusual in the consulting space — more common in fintech/SaaS. This positions ALISTA as a data-first firm, not a strategy boutique.

Cipher

Monogram — Badge Format
Visual Structure
A square badge with rounded corners. A geometric A letterform in Prestige Gold dominates the center. A partial C arc in Bright Cyan overlaps the right side — the two initials in dialogue. Border in Gold.
Symbolic Meaning
The A and C are not simply initials — they are a cipher, a locked system waiting to be unlocked. The incomplete C arc suggests that the engagement is ongoing, not closed. Gold = the premium value. Cyan = the technology layer.
Typographic Direction
The badge is the primary mark. The wordmark below is a supporting element in tracked DM Sans 500 in Gold — restrained, letterspaced, elegant.
Audience Resonance
The badge format reads as institutional and credentialed — like a firm that has been around long enough to earn a seal. Family business owners respond to signals of permanence and trust.
Competitive Differentiation
No regional competitor uses a badge/seal format. This positions ALISTA closer to a private equity firm or professional association than an IT consultancy — intentionally so.

Compass

Geometric Symbol
Visual Structure
A four-pointed star with one elongated north point in Electric Sapphire. Two horizontal cyan points extend east and west. A white circle at the center, with a gold dot as the compass needle. Pure geometric construction.
Symbolic Meaning
Strategy is navigation. The elongated north point is the direction — the singular outcome ALISTA aims at. The horizontal points represent the full operational scan (process, technology, people, data). The gold center is the client's position.
Typographic Direction
Syne 800 for "ALISTA" with Syne 400 for "CONSULTING" — same family, different weights. The weight contrast within one typeface is cleaner and more sophisticated than two font families.
Audience Resonance
Navigation and direction resonate universally with business owners who feel lost in technology choices. "You'll know where you're going" is a visceral promise.
Competitive Differentiation
The star/compass motif is common in strategy consulting globally but rare in Mexican regional IT consulting. At this level of execution — pure geometric, no gradients — it reads as internationally calibrated.

Clarity

Typographic Wordmark — Maximum Confidence
Visual Structure
"ALISTA" in Syne 800 at large scale in Electric Sapphire. A hairline cyan rule below the entire wordmark — a datum line, like a chart baseline. "CONSULTING" in wide-tracked DM Sans below in Platinum. No symbol.
Symbolic Meaning
The datum line is a measurement baseline — the starting point from which all savings are calculated. This is a consulting firm that frames everything as measurable from a known starting point. Pure data confidence.
Typographic Direction
The entire concept is typographic. Syne's geometric weight at display size has enough visual personality to function as a mark. The tracking contrast between "ALISTA" and "CONSULTING" creates hierarchy without a symbol.
Audience Resonance
For sophisticated buyers — second-generation family business owners, finance directors — a pure wordmark signals that the firm's name is strong enough to stand alone. No crutches. Confident.
Competitive Differentiation
Virtually every competitor in the regional space uses a symbol. A bold, well-executed wordmark immediately reads as a larger, more established firm. The absence of a symbol is the differentiator.

Color Psychology — What Each Color Signals to Non-Technical Buyers

Colors Are Arguments. Use Them Deliberately.

Midnight
#0A1628
Security, depth, authority. Dark backgrounds signal that the brand has nothing to prove. Buyers associate deep navy/midnight with financial institutions, private equity, and established firms. It says "we've been doing this."
Electric Sapphire
#2563EB
Trust, technology, action. Blue is the universal signal of reliability. The electric saturation elevates it beyond corporate blue — it feels active, not passive. CTAs in Sapphire say "this is the path."
Bright Cyan
#06B6D4
Innovation, speed, precision. Cyan reads as technical and forward-looking — it is the color of digital displays, laser cutting, and precision instruments. Used sparingly, it signals "we have something others don't."
Prestige Gold
#D4A843
Achievement, premium, earned status. Gold is psychologically associated with scarce resources and high value. Using it only for metrics, section numbers, and emphasis means that every time it appears, it carries weight.
Growth Green
#10B981
Money, growth, confirmation. Green is the universal color for "go" and "gain." In a consulting firm centered on savings, every positive metric in green is a subconscious reminder of the business case.
Typography Premium Rules

How Type Signals Authority at the Right Ratio

Rule
Use weight contrast, not size contrast.

A Syne 800 headline at 2rem next to a DM Sans 400 body at 1rem is more premium than a 4rem headline. Massive type screams for attention; weight contrast commands it quietly.

Rule
Wide tracking on small labels only.

Letter-spacing is premium at small sizes (eyebrows, tags, labels at 0.1–0.2em). Applied to large headings, it reads as weak. The distinction: precision tracking vs. compensatory tracking.

Rule
Monospace = data and precision.

IBM Plex Mono appears only for numbers, metrics, labels, and technical callouts. Every time it appears, it signals that the content is measured, not estimated.

Rule
Never center-align body text.

Centered body text is a design student habit. Left-aligned body text is how serious information is presented — in annual reports, financial disclosures, and premium brand guidelines.

10 Visual Rules for Premium Perception

The Rules That Separate Premium From Pretend

R01
Never fill more than 60% of a layout.

Negative space is not empty — it is confidence. Brands that need to fill every pixel look insecure. Premium brands breathe. Apply this to every touchpoint: proposals, social graphics, website sections.

R02
No more than 2 accent colors per UI block.

Pick the primary role (e.g., Sapphire for CTAs) and one supporting role (Green for metrics). Adding a third color breaks visual hierarchy and reads as amateur.

R03
Electric Sapphire occupies maximum 20% of any surface.

This color is the call-to-action color. Overuse kills its urgency. Apply it to interactive states, primary buttons, and key data points only.

R04
Prestige Gold is earned — never decorative.

Gold appears for: confirmed savings metrics, section/pillar numbers, award callouts, and premium emphasis. Never as a button color, background fill, or generic accent.

R05
Every positive metric uses Growth Green.

Savings figures, ROI percentages, successful implementation numbers — always in Growth Green. This conditions clients subconsciously: green = money saved = ALISTA.

R06
Icons are always line-style, never filled.

Filled icons read as early 2010s app design. Line icons read as precise, technical, considered. The negative space inside the line matches the brand's philosophy of restraint.

R07
Shadows are color-tinted, never pure black.

box-shadow using Deep Navy (#0F2440) at 30–50% opacity. Never rgba(0,0,0,x). Black shadows look cheap. Navy shadows look crafted.

R08
Typography: 3 size levels maximum per section.

Heading → Subhead → Body. Every additional level adds noise, not hierarchy. If you need a 4th level, the content architecture is broken, not the type system.

R09
Borders are 1px Platinum at 40% opacity.

rgba(232,236,241,0.40) on dark backgrounds. This creates structure without heaviness. A thick, opaque border on a dark card looks like a mistake. The hairline border looks intentional.

R10
Animate only transform and opacity. 150ms ease.

transition: transform 0.15s ease, opacity 0.15s ease. Never transition-all. Never animate color, background, or border-radius on hover. Motion is precision — not decoration.

Application Guidelines

How the Brand System Applies Across Touchpoints

Website

  • Dark mode by default (Midnight bg)
  • Sapphire CTAs, always full-width on mobile
  • Syne 800 for above-fold headline
  • Growth Green for all savings metrics
  • Logo: Primary horizontal, top-left
  • Subtle grain overlay at 3–4% opacity

LinkedIn & Social

  • Profile banner: Midnight bg + Vertex mark centered
  • Posts: 1:1 ratio, Deep Navy card bg
  • Stat posts: Large metric in Green/Gold (IBM Plex Mono)
  • Consistent Syne headline in every post
  • Max 2 colors per post graphic
  • No stock photo backgrounds

PDF Proposals

  • Light mode (Warm White bg) for printability
  • Midnight text, Sapphire headings
  • Gold for section numbers and callouts
  • Monochrome dark logo (top-left header)
  • ROI projections always in Growth Green table
  • Consistent DM Sans body, 10–11pt

Email Signature

  • Name: Syne 700 in Sapphire
  • Title: DM Sans 500, Platinum
  • Phone/email: IBM Plex Mono, Cyan label
  • Logo: Reversed/white on dark OR monochrome-dark on white
  • Divider: 1px Platinum 30%
  • No background color in signature block

Pitch Decks

  • Cover: Full-bleed Midnight with Vertex mark
  • Section dividers: Sapphire full-width rule
  • Data slides: Green for positive KPIs, always
  • Syne 800 for every section title
  • Discovery vs. Implementation: use 2 distinct slide styles
  • Last slide: positioning statement in Syne 800

Business Cards

  • Front: Midnight bg, Vertex icon centered, name in Syne
  • Back: Deep Navy with full logo + tagline in DM Sans
  • Spot Gold foil on the Vertex apex dot
  • Matte black finish, not glossy
  • Standard 3.5×2" — no unusual shapes
  • QR code to Discovery booking page (Cyan)
